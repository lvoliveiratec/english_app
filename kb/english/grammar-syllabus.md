# Grammar Syllabus

## A1 Grammar

- Subject pronouns: `I`, `you`, `he`, `she`, `it`, `we`, `they`.
- Be verb: `am`, `is`, `are`.
- Simple present affirmative, negative, and questions.
- Articles: `a`, `an`, `the`.
- Singular and plural nouns.
- Possessives: `my`, `your`, `his`, `her`, `our`, `their`.
- Demonstratives: `this`, `that`, `these`, `those`.
- Basic prepositions: `in`, `on`, `at`, `from`, `to`.
- There is / there are.
- Can / cannot for ability.

## A2 Grammar

- Simple past regular and common irregular verbs.
- Past questions and negatives with `did`.
- Future with `going to`.
- Future with `will`.
- Present continuous for now and future arrangements.
- Countable and uncountable nouns.
- Quantifiers: `some`, `any`, `much`, `many`, `a lot of`.
- Comparatives and superlatives.
- Modals: `should`, `must`, `have to`, `can`.
- Adverbs of frequency.

## B1 Grammar

- Present perfect with `ever`, `never`, `already`, `yet`, `for`, `since`.
- Present perfect vs simple past.
- Past continuous.
- Used to.
- First conditional.
- Zero conditional.
- Gerunds and infinitives.
- Relative clauses with `who`, `which`, `that`.
- Modals of possibility: `might`, `could`, `may`.
- Question tags.

## B2 Grammar

- Second conditional.
- Third conditional.
- Mixed conditionals.
- Passive voice across tenses.
- Reported speech.
- Reported questions.
- Defining and non-defining relative clauses.
- Modal perfect: `should have`, `could have`, `might have`.
- Advanced comparatives.
- Inversion for emphasis.

## C1-C2 Grammar

- Cleft sentences: `What I need is...`
- Advanced noun phrases.
- Participle clauses.
- Ellipsis and substitution.
- Formal and informal register control.
- Hedging: `It appears that`, `This may suggest`.
- Discourse markers: `nevertheless`, `moreover`, `in contrast`.
- Rhetorical questions.
- Parallel structure.
- Advanced punctuation for style and clarity.

## Correction Priority

When correcting grammar, prioritize:

1. Meaning-blocking errors.
2. Lesson target errors.
3. Recurring personal errors.
4. Errors that affect professional or academic tone.
5. Minor style improvements.
