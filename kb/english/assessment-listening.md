# Listening Assessment Guide (Text-Based)

## Purpose

Since the platform currently does not play audio, listening comprehension is assessed through
text-based simulations: transcript cloze, dictogloss reconstruction, and comprehension questions
based on short written dialogues or monologues formatted as transcripts.

These methods are widely validated in language assessment research and are an effective proxy
for real listening comprehension, especially for measuring grammar, vocabulary, and discourse
understanding in context.

---

## Method 1 — Transcript Cloze (recommended for A2–B2)

Present a short spoken-style transcript with blanks every 7–10 words. The student fills in the
missing words from context, or from a word bank for lower levels.

**Characteristics of spoken language to include:**
- Contractions: "I'm", "you're", "they've", "can't", "won't"
- Discourse markers: "well", "you know", "I mean", "actually", "so"
- Informal vocabulary: "a bit", "kind of", "loads of", "a lot"
- False starts and hedging: "I think...", "It's sort of..."

---

### A2 Cloze Sample

**Instructions:** Read the conversation and fill in the missing words.

> **Sarah:** Hi Tom! How __(1)__ your weekend?
> **Tom:** It was great! I __(2)__ to the beach with my family.
> **Sarah:** Oh nice! What was the __(3)__ like?
> **Tom:** Really hot. We __(4)__ swimming and then had ice cream.
> **Sarah:** That sounds __(5)__! I stayed home. I was a bit __(6)__.
> **Tom:** Oh no. Are you feeling better __(7)__?
> **Sarah:** Yes, much better, thanks __(8)__ asking!

**Answers:** (1) was, (2) went, (3) weather, (4) went, (5) wonderful/great/nice, (6) sick/ill/tired, (7) now, (8) for

**What to look for:** Does the student produce natural, contextually appropriate words? Using "wonderful" for (5) signals stronger vocabulary than "good".

---

### B1 Cloze Sample

**Instructions:** Fill in the missing words. The conversation is informal and natural.

> **Anna:** I can't believe she __(1)__ the job without even telling us she __(2)__ applying!
> **Mark:** I know. It's a bit __(3)__, isn't it? We were supposed to be a team.
> **Anna:** Exactly. I mean, I'm __(4)__ for her, but it still feels like she could have __(5)__ us.
> **Mark:** I think she was probably worried people __(6)__ be jealous or something.
> **Anna:** Maybe. I just wish she __(7)__ more open about it from the start.
> **Mark:** Well, what's done is done. We __(8)__ just be happy for her.

**Answers:** (1) got, (2) was, (3) strange/weird/odd, (4) happy/glad, (5) told/informed, (6) would, (7) had been, (8) should/ought to

**What to look for:** Complex tense usage (past perfect, second conditional, modals). Students placing correct tense forms score at B1+.

## Method 2 — Dictogloss (recommended for B1–B2)

Present a short spoken-style paragraph (80–120 words). The student reads it once quickly, then it is "removed" (or the student is told to cover it) and they must reconstruct the main ideas in their own words. This tests listening-level comprehension, grammar, and vocabulary simultaneously.

### B1 Dictogloss Sample

**Original passage (student reads once):**
> "A new study has found that people who exercise regularly tend to sleep better at night. Scientists believe this is because physical activity helps to reduce stress and improves the body's natural sleep cycle. The study looked at over 2,000 adults across different age groups. Participants who did at least 30 minutes of moderate exercise most days reported falling asleep faster and waking up less often during the night. Experts recommend walking, cycling, or swimming as effective options."

**After covering the text, ask:**
1. What did the study find? (main point)
2. Why does exercise help sleep? (cause and effect)
3. What type of exercise was recommended? (specific detail)
4. Who was included in the study? (detail)

**What to look for:** Does the student capture the core meaning accurately? Are they using their own words naturally? B1 students should get main point + 2 details. B2 students should get all 4.

## Method 3 — Dialogue Comprehension (recommended for A1–A2)

A short written dialogue in spoken style. Student answers simple questions about it.

### A1 Dialogue Sample

> **At a café:**
> **Waiter:** Hello! What would you like?
> **Customer:** Hi! Can I have a coffee, please?
> **Waiter:** Of course. Milk and sugar?
> **Customer:** Just milk, please. No sugar.
> **Waiter:** And anything to eat?
> **Customer:** No, thank you. Just the coffee.
> **Waiter:** That's $3.50. How would you like to pay?
> **Customer:** By card, please.

**Questions:**
1. Where is the customer? → In a café.
2. What does the customer order? → A coffee with milk and no sugar.
3. Does the customer order food? → No.
4. How does the customer pay? → By card.

## Spoken Language Features to Include at Each Level

| Level | Register | Vocabulary | Structure |
|---|---|---|---|
| A1–A2 | Very informal, face-to-face | Everyday words only | Short simple sentences, contractions |
| B1 | Informal to neutral | Some idiomatic phrases | Compound sentences, natural fillers |
| B2 | Neutral to semi-formal | Collocations, opinion language | Complex clauses, hedging, discourse markers |

---

## Scoring Guide

| Method | Pass Threshold |
|---|---|
| A2 Cloze (8 blanks) | 6/8 acceptable answers |
| B1 Cloze (8 blanks) | 5/8 with correct tense forms |
| B1 Dictogloss (4 questions) | 3/4 correct |
| B2 Dictogloss (4 questions) | 4/4 with precise vocabulary |
| A1 Dialogue (4 questions) | 3/4 correct |

---

## Notes for the AI Teacher

- In this platform, "listening" means simulated listening via written transcripts — be transparent with students: "We will test your ability to understand natural spoken English through transcripts."
- Students who score well on reading but poorly on cloze usually have vocabulary gaps in informal/spoken registers.
- Errors on contractions and informal markers (like "gonna", "kinda", "sort of") at B1 level suggest the student has mainly studied written or formal English.
- Cloze results feed directly into vocabulary and grammar placement — they are not a separate skill in isolation.
- In a future version with real audio, these transcripts become the basis for actual listening tasks without structural changes to the KB.
