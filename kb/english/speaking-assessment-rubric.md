# Speaking Assessment Rubric

Use this rubric to summarize student speaking practice.

## Fluency

1. Very limited: many long pauses; cannot complete simple ideas.
2. Emerging: frequent pauses; can complete short practiced sentences.
3. Developing: communicates familiar ideas with some hesitation.
4. Strong: speaks in connected sentences with manageable pauses.
5. Excellent: speaks smoothly and adapts language naturally.

## Pronunciation

1. Often unclear; listener needs frequent repetition.
2. Some words are clear; difficult sounds often block meaning.
3. Generally understandable; several recurring sound issues.
4. Clear most of the time; minor pronunciation issues remain.
5. Clear, controlled, and easy to understand.

## Grammar Accuracy

1. Very limited control of basic structures.
2. Basic sentence patterns are present but unstable.
3. Meaning is usually clear despite grammar mistakes.
4. Good control with occasional errors.
5. Accurate and flexible grammar use.

## Vocabulary Range

1. Very few words; relies on isolated vocabulary.
2. Basic everyday vocabulary.
3. Enough vocabulary for familiar topics.
4. Good range for many topics with some precision.
5. Wide, precise, and natural vocabulary.

## Interaction

1. Cannot respond without heavy support.
2. Responds to simple questions.
3. Maintains short conversations.
4. Asks follow-up questions and clarifies meaning.
5. Leads and adapts conversation naturally.

## Feedback Summary Format

Store compact summaries:

- Date.
- Activity.
- Level target.
- Strength.
- Main correction.
- Pronunciation target.
- Vocabulary target.
- Next recommended activity.
