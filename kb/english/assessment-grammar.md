# Grammar Assessment Guide

## Purpose

This guide defines which grammar structures to test at each CEFR level during placement.
Use it to generate grammar questions and evaluate student responses accurately.

## How to Use

- Select 2–3 structures from the target level to test.
- Use gap-fill or multiple-choice format (one correct answer, two plausible distractors).
- If the student answers correctly at level N, test level N+1.
- If the student answers incorrectly at level N, confirm at level N-1.

---

## A1 — Beginner

### Target structures
- Verb "to be" (I am, you are, he is)
- Simple present (I work, she studies)
- Present continuous (I am working)
- Basic questions (Where are you from? What do you do?)
- Imperatives (Open the door. Don't run.)
- There is / There are
- Possessive adjectives (my, your, his, her)
- Basic modals: can for ability (I can swim)
- Numbers, days, months, time expressions

### Sample gap-fill items (A1)
- "She ___ a teacher." → is
- "They ___ football every weekend." → play
- "I ___ speak English a little." → can
- "There ___ two chairs in the room." → are
- "___ you from Brazil?" → Are

---

## A2 — Elementary

### Target structures (builds on A1)
- Simple past (I went, she studied, he didn't go)
- Past continuous (I was watching TV when she called)
- Present perfect for experience (Have you ever been to London?)
- Going to for plans (I am going to visit my parents)
- Will for predictions (It will rain tomorrow)
- Countable and uncountable nouns (some water, a few books)
- Comparatives and superlatives (taller, the tallest)
- Must / have to for obligation
- Should for advice
- Definite and indefinite articles (a, an, the)
- Simple phrasal verbs (get up, turn on, look for)
- Zero and first conditional (If it rains, I will stay home)

### Sample gap-fill items (A2)
- "I ___ to Paris last year." → went
- "She ___ (study) when the phone rang." → was studying
- "Have you ever ___ sushi?" → eaten
- "You ___ drink more water. It is good for you." → should
- "This is ___ most beautiful city I have ever seen." → the

---

## B1 — Intermediate

### Target structures (builds on A2)
- Past perfect (She had already left when I arrived)
- Future continuous (I will be working at 3pm)
- Second conditional (If I had more time, I would travel)
- Reported speech (He said he was tired. She asked where I lived.)
- Present perfect continuous (I have been studying for two hours)
- Simple passive voice (The letter was written by her)
- Modal perfects: should have, might have, could have
- First, second, and review of zero conditional
- Relative clauses with who, which, that (The man who called is my uncle)
- Linking words: although, however, therefore, because, so that

### Sample gap-fill items (B1)
- "If I ___ more money, I would buy a new car." → had
- "The report ___ by the manager last week." → was written
- "She said she ___ tired." → was
- "I ___ (study) English for three years now." → have been studying
- "He ___ (call) me — I missed an important meeting." → should have called

---

## B2 — Upper Intermediate

### Target structures (builds on B1)
- Mixed conditionals (If she had studied harder, she would be a doctor now)
- Third conditional (If he had arrived on time, we wouldn't have missed the train)
- Future perfect (By next year, I will have finished the course)
- Past perfect continuous (She had been working for hours before she stopped)
- Complex passive forms (It is believed that... / The problem is said to be...)
- Wish structures (I wish I knew the answer. I wish I had studied more.)
- Defining and non-defining relative clauses
- Inversion for emphasis (Never have I seen such a thing)
- Advanced discourse markers: nevertheless, in spite of, as a result, on the other hand
- Reported questions and commands

### Sample gap-fill items (B2)
- "If she ___ harder at school, she would be a lawyer now." → had studied
- "By the time they arrive, we ___ (finish) dinner." → will have finished
- "I wish I ___ (know) the answer to that question." → knew
- "It ___ that the company will close next month." → is reported
- "Never ___ I seen such a beautiful landscape." → have

---

## C1 — Advanced

### Target structures (builds on B2)
- Inversion for emphasis (Never have I..., Not only did she..., Should you need...)
- Cleft sentences (It was the price that surprised me. What I need is more time.)
- Nominalization (The announcement of the decision caused concern.)
- Advanced subjunctive and formal conditionals (Were I to resign... / Had they arrived earlier...)
- Complex noun phrases and post-modification (The proposal submitted by the committee...)
- Hedging and epistemic modals (It would appear that... / This may well be the case...)
- Discourse-level connectors (Nevertheless, by the same token, in so far as...)
- Reporting verbs with complex patterns (She insisted on being told. He denied having taken it.)
- Advanced passive constructions (It is widely believed... / The results are said to indicate...)

### Sample gap-fill items (C1)
- "___ I known about the problem earlier, I could have prevented it." → Had
- "It was ___ the project failed that the team lost confidence." → only when / only after
- "___ she refuse to cooperate, we will have no choice but to escalate." → Should
- "The decision ___ by the board came as a shock to employees." → reached / taken / made
- "Not only ___ she miss the meeting, but she also failed to send a report." → did

## Scoring Guide

| Score (correct out of 2 per level) | Assessment |
|---|---|
| 0–1 at A1 | Confirm A1, begin at Beginner |
| 2 at A1, 0–1 at A2 | Place at A1–A2 boundary (Elementary) |
| 2 at A2, 0–1 at B1 | Place at A2 (Elementary confirmed) |
| 2 at A2, 2 at B1, 0–1 at B2 | Place at B1 (Intermediate) |
| 2 at B1, 2 at B2 | Place at B2 or above |

## Important Notes

- Never ask more than 8 grammar questions total in a placement session.
- Always pair grammar assessment with vocabulary and writing to triangulate the level.
- If the student self-reports "Advanced" but scores at B1 grammar, trust the test over the self-report.
- Be encouraging in feedback: frame results as a starting point, not a judgment.
