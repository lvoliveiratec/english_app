# Vocabulary Assessment Guide

## Purpose

This guide defines vocabulary ranges and assessment formats for each CEFR level.
Use it to generate vocabulary questions and interpret student responses during placement.

## Vocabulary Ranges by Level

Based on Nation's Vocabulary Levels framework and CEFR alignment:

| Level | Word Families | Coverage of Everyday Speech |
|---|---|---|
| A1 | ~500 | Basic survival communication |
| A2 | ~1,000 | Simple routine topics |
| B1 | ~2,000–3,000 | Familiar everyday situations |
| B2 | ~4,000–6,000 | ~95–98% of spoken everyday English |
| C1 | ~8,000+ | Academic and professional contexts |

---

## Question Formats

### Format 1 — Multiple Choice (receptive, easiest)
> "Which word means 'very tired'?"
> a) exhausted  b) excited  c) confused  d) relaxed
> **Answer: a**

Best for: A1–A2. Fast to answer, tests recognition.

### Format 2 — Gap Fill (productive, moderate)
> "She felt ___ after working 12 hours without a break."
> **Answer: exhausted / tired / drained**

Best for: A2–B2. Tests whether the student can retrieve words in context.

### Format 3 — Word Definition Match
> "Match the word to its meaning: *annual*, *colleague*, *purchase*"
> "happening every year / someone you work with / to buy something"
> **Answers: annual = happening every year, colleague = someone you work with, purchase = to buy**

Best for: B1–B2. Tests both breadth and precision.

### Format 4 — Odd One Out
> "Which word does not belong? *kitchen, bedroom, hospital, bathroom*"
> **Answer: hospital (not a room in a house)**

Best for: A2–B1. Tests category knowledge and conceptual grouping.

## Vocabulary by Level — Topic Reference

### A1 — Core Survival Vocabulary
- **People:** family, friend, teacher, doctor
- **Places:** home, school, shop, bank, hospital
- **Actions:** go, come, eat, drink, work, study, live, have, want, need
- **Descriptions:** big, small, old, new, good, bad, hot, cold
- **Numbers, colors, days, months**
- **Common adjectives:** happy, sad, hungry, tired

### A2 — Everyday Topics
- **Daily life:** routine, transport, shopping, food, weather
- **Work:** job, office, meeting, salary, colleague
- **Feelings:** nervous, excited, bored, surprised, angry, worried
- **Travel:** airport, hotel, ticket, passport, departure, arrival
- **Health:** headache, medicine, appointment, exercise
- **Linking words:** but, because, so, and, or, also, then

### B1 — Extended Familiar Topics
- **Work and career:** deadline, responsibility, experience, promotion, skills
- **Environment:** pollution, energy, recycle, climate, natural disaster
- **Relationships:** trust, support, argument, communication, respect
- **Technology:** device, application, connection, update, download
- **News and society:** government, economy, crime, education, immigration
- **Academic words:** research, evidence, suggest, method, result, process

### B2 — Abstract and Professional Topics
- **Business:** negotiate, budget, strategy, investment, revenue, client
- **Society and politics:** democracy, policy, reform, inequality, welfare
- **Science and technology:** innovation, artificial intelligence, data, algorithm
- **Academic register:** analyze, evaluate, hypothesis, conclusion, perspective
- **Idiomatic language:** "hit the nail on the head", "burn the midnight oil", "see eye to eye"
- **Collocations:** "make a decision", "take responsibility", "reach a conclusion"

## Sample Question Sets by Level

### A1 Vocabulary Questions (2–3 questions)
1. "Which word means a person who teaches?" → teacher
2. "What do you say when you meet someone for the first time?" → "Nice to meet you" / "Hello"
3. Gap fill: "I ___ (be) hungry. I want to eat." → am

### A2 Vocabulary Questions (2–3 questions)
1. "Choose the correct word: She was very ___ before her job interview. (relaxed / nervous / bored)" → nervous
2. Gap fill: "I need to buy a ___ for the flight." → ticket
3. "Which word does not belong? coffee, tea, juice, bread" → bread (not a drink)

### B1 Vocabulary Questions (2–3 questions)
1. "What does 'deadline' mean?" → the latest time something must be finished
2. Gap fill: "The scientists found strong ___ that the new drug works." → evidence
3. "Choose the correct collocation: She had to ___ a difficult decision." → make (not do/take)

### B2 Vocabulary Questions (2–3 questions)
1. "What does 'negotiate' mean in a business context?" → to discuss something to reach an agreement
2. Gap fill: "The government needs to ___ (= create and announce) a new policy on immigration." → implement / introduce
3. "What does the idiom 'see eye to eye' mean?" → to agree with someone

## Evaluation Notes

- A student who answers A2 vocabulary correctly but struggles with B1 words → place at A2.
- A student who answers all B1 vocabulary correctly → test B2 vocabulary.
- Rich, varied vocabulary in writing samples (idioms, collocations, B2+ words) is strong evidence for B2 or above even if grammar has some errors.
- Vocabulary errors are often more revealing than grammar errors for placement: a student who uses "exhausted" instead of "tired" demonstrates B1+ active vocabulary.
- Do not penalize for minor spelling errors in productive tasks (gap fill, writing).
