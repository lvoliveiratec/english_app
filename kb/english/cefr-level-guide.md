# CEFR Level Guide

## A1 Beginner

The student can understand and use familiar everyday expressions and basic phrases.

Teacher behavior:

- Use short sentences.
- Use common words.
- Ask one question at a time.
- Correct only the most important mistake.
- Use examples before explanations.

Expected skills:

- Introduce self.
- Ask and answer basic personal questions.
- Use simple present with common verbs.
- Understand slow, clear speech.
- Write simple sentences about self, family, work, and routine.

Common targets:

- Be verbs: `I am`, `you are`, `he is`.
- Simple present: `I work`, `she studies`.
- Basic questions: `Where are you from?`
- Numbers, days, time, family, jobs, food, places.

## A2 Elementary

The student can communicate in simple routine tasks and describe familiar topics.

Teacher behavior:

- Keep English simple but natural.
- Start building longer answers.
- Encourage full sentences.
- Review past and future forms often.

Expected skills:

- Talk about daily routines and past experiences.
- Make simple plans.
- Ask for help or clarification.
- Understand short conversations.
- Write short messages and descriptions.

Common targets:

- Simple past: `I went`, `I studied`.
- Future with `going to` and `will`.
- Countable and uncountable nouns.
- Comparatives and superlatives.
- Common modal verbs: `can`, `should`, `must`.

## B1 Intermediate

The student can handle familiar situations, explain opinions, and describe experiences.

Teacher behavior:

- Use natural English with support.
- Ask follow-up questions.
- Correct recurring grammar and vocabulary patterns.
- Push the student to explain reasons.

Expected skills:

- Maintain conversations on familiar topics.
- Give opinions and reasons.
- Tell stories with sequence.
- Understand main ideas in clear speech.
- Write connected paragraphs.

Common targets:

- Present perfect.
- Past continuous.
- First conditional.
- Modal verbs for advice and obligation.
- Linking words: `because`, `although`, `however`, `so`.

## B2 Upper Intermediate

The student can communicate with fluency on a wide range of topics and understand complex texts.

Teacher behavior:

- Use more natural speed and phrasing.
- Correct nuance, collocations, and sentence flow.
- Challenge vague answers.
- Introduce idiomatic but practical language.

Expected skills:

- Discuss abstract and concrete topics.
- Defend opinions.
- Participate in meetings and interviews.
- Understand extended speech.
- Write clear structured texts.

Common targets:

- Second and third conditionals.
- Passive voice.
- Reported speech.
- Relative clauses.
- Advanced connectors.
- Register and tone.

## C1 Advanced

The student can use English flexibly for social, academic, and professional purposes.

Teacher behavior:

- Focus on precision, nuance, and style.
- Give targeted feedback.
- Use authentic materials.
- Encourage self-correction.

Expected skills:

- Speak fluently with few pauses.
- Adjust tone to audience.
- Understand implicit meaning.
- Write persuasive, organized, and nuanced texts.
- Use a wide range of vocabulary naturally.

Common targets:

- Advanced discourse markers.
- Hedging and diplomacy.
- Idiomatic language.
- Complex noun phrases.
- Academic and professional style.

## C2 Proficient

The student can understand almost everything and express meaning precisely.

Teacher behavior:

- Act more like an editor, debate partner, and fluency coach.
- Focus on polish, rhetorical control, and specialized language.
- Use high-level authentic content.

Expected skills:

- Speak spontaneously and precisely.
- Handle complex arguments.
- Understand subtle humor, tone, and implication.
- Write with strong control of style and structure.

Common targets:

- Rhetorical precision.
- Advanced collocations.
- Field-specific vocabulary.
- Persuasive speaking.
- Editorial writing.
